Overview
Dice Towers is a fast-paced stacking game of skill, risk, laughter and collapse. Players take turns rolling dice to determine which of two shared towers — a white tower and a black tower — they must add to, and which numbered die to place.

Towers grow taller, wobble and eventually fall. The player who built wisely scores big. The player who knocked it over loses points. First to 100 points wins — or the leader when 15 minutes is up.

Components
36 Coloured Dice — varied sizes (14mm–30mm)
24 Pool Dice (12 white, 12 black)
2 Game Dice (1× D6, 1× White/Black die)
2 Leader Markers
2 Tower Bases (white & black)
50 Scoresheets

Player Dice

Each player has 6 dice in their own colour, numbered 1–6 — where each die has the same number on all six faces. The size of the die matches its number: the 1-die is the smallest (14mm) and the 6-die is the largest (30mm). This means every stacking choice has physical consequences.

How to Play

Setup

  • Place the white and black tower bases in the centre of the table, next to each other.
  • Each player selects a set of 6 dice in their chosen colour.
  • Arrange the pool dice: 2 white and 2 black dice of each number (1–6) face-up around the bases.
  • Nominate one player to keep score on the scoresheet.

Turn Sequence

On each turn, a player rolls both Game Dice simultaneously:

  • The D6 determines the number of the die to be placed.
  • The White/Black die determines which tower to place on.
Example: Roll a 3 and White → you must place a 3 on the white tower.

Placing a Die

  • You may place your own coloured die of the rolled number, OR the matching pool die of the correct tower colour.
  • The die must be placed carefully — it forms part of the growing stack.
  • You cannot skip unless no valid move is possible.
Strategic choice: placing a pool die keeps your own dice safe — but gives your opponents nothing to score off!

Passing Rules

  • If you can play but choose not to (too risky!) → lose 2 points.
  • If you have no coloured die of the required number AND no matching pool die → you may play any pool die of the tower colour, or pass for no penalty.
  • If you have your coloured die of the rolled number but no pool die → you must play it, or pass for −2 points.

Tower Stability & Rebuilding

  • As towers grow, they become increasingly unstable.
  • If a tower falls, it is immediately scored — but the game continues with the other tower still in play.
  • On the next turn someone rolls that tower's colour, it is rebuilt from scratch — pool dice reset to 2 of each number, and players get their coloured dice back.
  • The current tower leader carries a Leader Marker so points are always clear when a tower falls.
Scoring
⬜ White Tower

When the white tower falls, the highest coloured die on the tower at the moment it fell determines the scorer — that player earns:

Top Die Value × Total Dice in Tower

The player who knocked it over loses 5 points.

Example: Blue 4 on top, 9 dice in tower → Blue player scores 4 × 9 = 36 points. Knocker loses 5.
⬛ Black Tower

When the black tower falls, all players score the sum of their own coloured dice placed on the tower (black pool dice don't count). The player whose coloured die was placed last gets that sum as their score.

Sum of all coloured dice on tower

The player who knocked it over loses 5 points.

Example: Dice of 2, 3, 5, 4, 2, 3 on tower → last placer scores 2+3+5+4+2+3 = 19 points.
🔥 Both Towers Fall

On rare occasions, knocking one tower causes the other to fall too. Score each tower independently using the rules above. However, the player responsible for the chaos:

LOSES 20 POINTS

This is the price of spectacular destruction.

Additional Scoring Notes

  • Passing when you could play: −2 points
  • Knocking over a single tower: −5 points
  • Knocking over both towers: −20 points
  • Passing when no valid die exists: no penalty
End Game
100

Points Victory

The first player to reach 100 points wins immediately. Keep the scoresheets close — it can happen fast!

15

Time Victory

If 15 minutes pass before anyone reaches 100, the player with the most points at that moment wins.

Strategic Tips

Use Pool Dice Wisely

You can place your own coloured die or a pool die — but placing a pool die means other players get nothing to score off your work. Sometimes the smart play is letting the tower grow on someone else's dice.

Stability is Key

Pay attention to how your die affects the tower's balance. Larger dice at the bottom gives stability; a big die near the top is a ticking time bomb — for you or someone else.

Timing & Positioning

Place higher-numbered dice toward the top of the white tower to maximise your score when it falls — but be careful. An unbalanced tower can come down on your own turn.

Know When to Pass

Passing costs 2 points — but sometimes paying 2 to avoid being the one who sends 9 dice flying is worth it. Know the difference between cowardice and clever risk management.
